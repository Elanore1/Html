Evening House, all the activities to do with others!

You will find below all our ideas of games to do in the evening, whether with friends, with the family or even as a couple. You will find all our tips and tricks to keep you busy with the least amount of material possible. You can adapt the rules of the game with your own ideas.


GUESSING



The Alarme Game


1- A player determines a time slot that will arrive in a short time.
For example, if it is 10:23 pm, he will say "between 10:30 and 11 pm".

2-All players set an alarm on their phone in this time slot without showing it to others.

3- The goal of the game is not to make the alarm ring first or last.

The first and the last will drink 3 sips (or a token for the little ones)




CARD


The President

Players: 4-6
Material: a deck of 52 cards.

All the cards are dealt and the one who has the queen of hearts starts.
It is played clockwise. The goal of the game is to have no more cards in your hand.
The cards are ranked as follows:
| 2 | Ace | King | Queen | Jack | 10 | 9 | ... | 3 | with 2 being the best card and 3 the lowest.



  If Player 1 lays down a single card, Player 2 (and subsequent players) must lay down a higher card (regardless of suit).

  If Player 1 lays down a pair, Player 2 (and subsequent players) must also play with two cards that have the same number but are still higher than
  the previous cards. The same rule applies for three and four cards of the same number.



  In the President there are ranks:

  - The president who is the winner of the previous game.
  - The vice president or minister is the one who came second.
  - The vice asshole who is the second last.
  - The asshole who is the loser of the previous game.

  -> The president will give his two least advantageous cards to the asshole, and the asshole will give his two best cards to the president for the next
  game.
  -> The vice president will give a less advantageous card to the vice asshole who in turn will give him his best card for the next game.



BLUFF

  The Post-it Game :



A classic of evenings with friends or family, the post-it game is simple.

You have to write on a post-it the name of a character, and stick it on the forehead of your right neighbor.
The goal is to guess the name of the personality.
The youngest player starts by asking a question.
If the player gets a "yes" answer, he or she can continue with another question.
If the answer is "no", it is the right-hand neighbor's turn to ask a question... until the table is full.
Between 3 and 8 players are needed for this game.
