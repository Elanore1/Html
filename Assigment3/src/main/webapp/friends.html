SPENDING A NIGHT WITH FRIENDS : WHAT TO DO ?

          


The COVID health crisis has shown us how much we really need to get together physically with our entourage and create social links. In order to make the most of these shared moments of relaxation, we propose here some original ways to organize a party with friends. You don't need a big budget or a new place to spice up your reunion. Just a little creativity to spend an evening with friends that is out of the ordinary. Come on, follow us, let's go for a laugh and have fun every time!


1 - Karaoke nights :


You can't imagine all the psychological benefits of karaoke on the morale in terms of stress reduction, self-confidence reinforcement, etc.

So, let's not deprive ourselves of it!
The success of this party depends first of all on a good preparation beforehand. Install quality sound equipment in advance. Then, plan different types of playlists: modern sounds as well as classics from the French or international repertoire; songs with lyrics as well as funny ones, even bawdy ones... In short, vary the themes to change the rhythm!


    You can also create teams between your guests, just to challenge the interpretations.
    Involve them more by making them choose their own music titles, by a draw for example.
    You place pieces of paper on which you have written the titles of the songs in a container,
    and you ask each guest, in turn, to draw one at random.
    Enjoy yourself and have fun!



    

2 - Wraps party ! :


What better way to spend a night out with friends than making wraps?

Everyone can afford to choose what they put in them depending on their taste. A wonderful evening with friends.

3 - Mojito :



The Mojito is a traditional cocktail of Cuban cuisine and culture made with rum, soda, lime and fresh mint leaves. It was born in Cuba in the Caribbean in the 1910s.


Legend has it that the history of the mojito dates back to the beginning of the 16th century when the English privateer explorer Francis Drake, between two raids on Havana, enjoyed sipping mint leaves crushed with tafia (industrial rum). This tradition explains why customers ask in some bars "give me a Drake".
